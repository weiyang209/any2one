Core Features

Built for real research workflows

From multi-source input to quality review, every step is designed for real-world research needs.

🧠
Multi-Topic Splitting
Long meetings are automatically analyzed for independent topics. Each topic generates its own research report with clear boundaries.
🎚️
Controllable Depth
Choose from simple / medium / complex modes to control literature scope, source opening, and research cost.
🔎
Directed Research
Candidate queries are generated before retrieval. Confirm, add, or refine them before search begins to reduce waste.
🧪
Multi-Model Review
Review → Rewrite loops with multi-dimensional quality diagnosis and bounded revision until the quality gate is passed.

Research Depth

Choose your research depth

Match the research mode to your task — balance coverage, cost, and report depth.

MODE · simple
Quick Overview
Rapidly understand background and supplement core related work. Ideal for lightweight briefings or initial exploration.
3–5 papers ≥3 opened 2 recent
MODE · medium
Balanced Research
Balances coverage, cost, and report depth. Recommended as the default research mode for most tasks.
6–10 papers ≥6 opened 4 recent
MODE · complex
Deep Research
For cross-domain, complex problems, or tasks requiring systematic literature mapping. Produces in-depth research reports.
10–15 papers ≥10 opened 6 recent

Input Support

Supported input formats

Audio, video, documents, spreadsheets, slides, archives, and online links — automatically identified and routed to the appropriate pipeline.

🎙️
Meeting Audio
→ Structured meeting notes
.mp3 .wav .m4a
WhisperX speech recognition with speaker diarization, auto-generating structured 7-segment notes with speaker attribution.
🎬
Meeting Video
→ Structured meeting notes
.mp4 .mov .mkv
ffmpeg extracts the audio stream and pipes it to the meeting-audio-grounding pipeline for full structured meeting notes.
📄
Documents
→ Structured research notes
.pdf .docx .md .txt
Docling deeply extracts text, tables, figures, equations, and code blocks, producing an 8-segment structured note.
📊
Spreadsheet Data
→ Data analysis notes
.xlsx .csv
Extracts schema, statistical summaries, preview rows, and auto-generates visualizations. Identifies outliers and data quality issues.
📑
Presentations
→ Presentation structure notes
.pptx
Extracts slide structure, titles, text, figures, tables, and speaker notes. Analyzes narrative flow and key signals.
📦
Archives
→ Archive-level summary notes
.zip
Unpacks and identifies each subfile type, parallel-calls corresponding grounding skills, and generates a cross-file summary note.

Quick Start

Get started

Three steps from raw input to complete research report.

one-report · prompt example 
# Send this prompt to the Cursor Agent

Please use .cursor/skills/one-report/
to generate a full report.

Input:
  input_path: docs/showcase/inputs/case5/HER2-case5.zip
  output_formats: pdf
  research_mode: complex

Search settings:
  search_backend:  cursor
  require_open_link: true
  download_opened_literature: true

Optional:
  output_lang: zh
  transcription_language: zh
1

Prepare your input file

Place files in data/raw_inputs/. Supports audio, video, documents, spreadsheets, PPT, archives, or paste a link directly.

2

Send prompt to Cursor Agent

Read .cursor/skills/one-report/SKILL.md and fill in input_path, research_mode, and output_formats as shown.

3

Wait for full pipeline execution

Agent automatically runs grounding → research → summary → review → export end-to-end.
